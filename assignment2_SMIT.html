HOW FAR IS MY EXPERIENCE WITH SMIT? | IS IT REALLY WORTH IT
My journey with SMIT started just three weeks ago, and to be honest, it already feels like a turning point in my learning path. When I first enrolled, I wasn’t sure how the experience would turn out. Like most students, I had my doubts—would it be worth my time? Would I really be able to stay consistent? But now, looking back at these three weeks, I can confidently say that my experience has been nothing short of amazing.
From the very beginning, the environment has been welcoming and motivating. The instructors explain concepts step by step, making sure no one is left behind. The way the course is structured keeps me engaged, and every new class builds smoothly on what we learned before. It doesn’t feel rushed, yet at the same time, the pace is strong enough to push us forward without losing interest.
In just three weeks, we have almost completed HTML, which is a huge achievement in itself. Before joining, I only had a basic idea about web development, but now I can confidently write and understand HTML structure, tags, and elements. The assignments and practical exercises are very helpful because they don’t just test my knowledge—they make me apply it, which is the real way to learn.
One of the best parts about SMIT is the sense of progress. Every day I attend, I feel like I’m moving closer to becoming the developer I want to be. The small wins, like writing my first proper webpage or understanding how HTML works behind the scenes, keep me motivated. It’s not just about learning a skill; it’s about building confidence step by step.
So, is SMIT really worth it? For me, the answer is a big yes. These three weeks have shown me that this decision was the right one. The way things are going, I am excited to move ahead into CSS, JavaScript, and beyond. If the first three weeks can make me this confident and involved, I can only imagine how much stronger my skills will be by the end of the course.
In short, my experience with SMIT so far has been completely worth it. It has given me direction, focus, and a strong foundation to grow as a developer. I’m glad I took this step, and I’m excited to see how far I can go from here.
